Notre Dame

At Notre Dame, I'm pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering. I chose computer engineering (CPEG) as opposed to CS because I'm equally interested in hardware and software. CPEG at Notre Dame is pretty much just CS but you take 4 EE classes instead of theory-heavy CS classes, which I like. At a lot of schools CPEG is basically EE with a couple CS classes, but at Notre Dame it's the opposite. I'd still recommend it over EE because EE's have to take some tough courses that aren't super interesting (in my opinion), and just have a generally harder curriculum.

The CPEG workload isn't too bad, but it definitely ramps up around sophomore spring to end of junior year which is when you take most of the required classes. Past that it's mostly just electives and a capstone class. If you manage your time well, you'll be completely fine and won't spend much a crazy amount of time outside of class doing work.

I got to study abroad in London🇬🇧 over my freshman summer, and it was an amazing experience. It got me two classes ahead, and I had an awesome time. I'd highly recommend going abroad in the summer if you can, the Engineering programs are super cool: London, Dublin, Berlin, Alcoy (Spain), and more. It's nice to study abroad in the summer because you don't have to move any classes around or worry about missing anything on campus. I also studied abroad in Dublin my junior fall (2025) for a semester at Dublin City University. This program is built for CSE students so it didn't put me behind at all. I'd highly recommend it I had an amazing time and it was coincidentally my easiest semester and my highest graded semester, for what it's worth.

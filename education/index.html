Notre Dame

At Notre Dame, I'm pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering with a focus on robotics and autonomous systems. I chose computer engineering (CPEG) as opposed to CS because I'm equally interested in hardware and software. CPEG at Notre Dame is pretty much just CS but you take 4 EE classes instead of more theoretical CS classes, which I like. At a lot of schools CPEG is basically EE with a couple CS classes, but at Notre Dame it's the opposite. I'd still recommend it over EE because EE's have to take a ton of super hard classes that aren't super interesting (in my opinion), and just have a generally harder curriculum.

I got to study abroad in London over my freshman summer, and it was an amazing experience. It got me two classes ahead, and I had an awesome time. I'd highly recommend it if you can. If you care, below are the courses I've taken so far (you probably don't).
